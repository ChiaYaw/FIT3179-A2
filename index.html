Victimisation in Australia from 2008 to 2022

Victimisation refers to the process of being made a victim
of a crime or unfair treatment. It involves individuals experiencing
harm, suffering, or loss due to the actions of others.
Victimisation can occur in various forms, such as
physical assault, theft, or emotional abuse.

Victimisation Rate by State/Territory and Crime Type

Physical Assault:
A person is physically attacked or harmed.

Direct Assault:
A person is threatened with harm in a face-to-face encounter.

Indirect Assault:
Threats of harm made without a face-to-face interaction, such as through phone calls, messages, or other indirect means.

Combined Threats:
Both face-to-face and non face-to-face threatened assaults. If a person has experienced both types, they are counted separately for each but only once in the total

Combined Assaults:
All instances of physical assault and threatened assault. If a person has experienced both physical and threatened assault, they are counted separately for each type but only once in the total
