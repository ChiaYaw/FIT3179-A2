Victimisation in Australia

Victimisation refers to the process of being made a victim
of a crime or unfair treatment. It involves individuals experiencing
harm, suffering, or loss due to the actions of others.
Victimisation can occur in various forms, such as
physical assault, theft, or emotional abuse.

Victimisation rate refers to the percentage of individuals who have
experienced a particular crime within a specified period. This map
provides a visual representation of the victimisation rates across different
states and territories in Australia for the year 2022. A
victimisation rate of 1.3% means that 1.3 out of every
100 individuals have experienced a particular crime, such as physical

assault, within the specified period. To put this into perspective,
countries with lower victimisation rates, such as Japan, often report
rates below 1%, indicating a relatively safer environment. On the
other hand, some countries with higher crime rates may report
timisation rates above 5%, highlighting more significant safety. This comparison
helps us understand the relative safety of different regions within Australia.
